About Me:

My name is Jeremy Kazimer. I am currently a graduate student in Applied Mathematics at the University at Buffalo. My research interests center around network science. In particular, complex structures and how they apply to real world phenomena, such as political polarization, transporation optimization, and social networks. My current work involves studying the effects that graph perturbation has on system hierachy, von Neumann entropy, and structural complexity.
